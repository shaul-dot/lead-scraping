## EXCLUSION CATEGORIES (the only reasons to mark qualified: false)

**1. Pure product / e-commerce brand**
- Selling physical products with no coaching, service, or expertise offering attached
- Examples: supplement brands (no coaching), clothing, equipment, food products
- EXCEPTION: if there's any coaching/service/program component, even as upsell or bundle, mark qualified: true

**2. SaaS / software / mobile app**
- Selling access to software, apps, tools, or platforms as the primary product
- Examples: productivity apps, CRM software, tracking apps, AI tools, fintech tools, developer tools, indie-maker SaaS
- This exclusion holds EVEN when the founder has a strong personal brand, newsletter, podcast, or book
- EXCEPTION: if the software is paired with a separate coaching program, course, or consulting offering (not just a software feature), qualified: true

**3. Local-service-only business**
- Physical location-dependent service with no online offering
- Examples: dentists, salons, restaurants, gyms, chiropractors (without online programs), auto repair, plastic surgery practices
- EXCEPTION: if they have any online course, program, or coaching offering, mark qualified: true

**4. Media / entertainment / news / pure creator**
- Monetizes only through ad revenue, sponsorships, or free content
- No paid offering of their own
- Examples: news outlets, entertainment channels, newsletters monetized via sponsorships, influencers without a paid product
- EXCEPTION: if they sell a course, book, membership, coaching, etc., mark qualified: true

**5. Affiliate marketer (as primary business)**
- Promoting someone else's product/program for commission as the PRIMARY business model
- Signal: landing page or Linktree is dominantly affiliate links to third-party retailers; no meaningful offering of their own
- NOTE: many legitimate coaches have some affiliate links. Only exclude when affiliate is clearly the primary business.

**6. Non-commercial organization**
- Church services, non-profits, government-funded social services agencies, community groups with no paid consumer offering
- Free events with no product/service attached
- Donations-only is NOT the same as selling a service

**7. Enterprise-only B2B**
- Explicitly and exclusively targets Fortune 500 / large enterprise clients with RFP-driven sales motion
- Signal: landing page emphasizes "Fortune 500", "Enterprise", "RFP", names only major corporate clients, no self-service or SMB offering
- EXCEPTION: B2B consultants/agencies serving SMBs, startups, or mid-market → qualified: true. Mixed enterprise + SMB/startup clientele also qualifies. Only truly enterprise-only gets excluded.

**8. Regulated financial services**
- Businesses whose primary revenue is from regulated financial products or services: investment management, fund sponsorship (ETFs, mutual funds), brokerage, registered investment advisory (RIA), insurance sales, lending, crypto/securities trading platforms, financial planning with AUM fees, robo-advisors
- Exclude even when the founder is a personal-brand thought leader with a book, podcast, newsletter, or speaking circuit
- KEY DISTINCTION: Financial EDUCATORS who sell only courses/coaching/community about money (no AUM, no licensed advisory, no securities products) → qualified: true. Unlicensed trading mentors selling Discord/community → qualified: true.

**9. Service-delivery agencies (non-marketing, non-consulting)**
- Agencies that sell labor/execution rather than expertise
- Examples: recruitment/staffing agencies, talent/casting/modeling agencies, travel agencies, real estate agencies (selling houses), AI implementation/automation build agencies, software dev shops, video production/photography agencies, translation agencies, pure graphic design agencies
- RULE OF THUMB: If an agency sells primarily TIME and LABOR to execute tasks, exclude. If they sell STRATEGY, EXPERTISE, or PLAYBOOKS (client pays for know-how), include.

**10. Non-English ad copy**
- The ad itself is primarily in a language other than English
- EXCEPTION: If the ad copy is English, qualify even if they serve bilingual/multilingual audiences. Language of the AD determines qualification, not the audience they serve.
