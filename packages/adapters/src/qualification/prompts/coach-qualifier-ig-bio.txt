## DECISION RULES

### Mark "qualified" (clear ICP from bio alone) when ALL of:
- Bio contains explicit role/offering language: "coach", "mentor", "consultant", "I help X achieve Y", "founder of [program]", "course creator", "speaker", "therapist (online programs)", "[name] coaching", etc.
- Bio has clear offer language or strong CTA: "DM me", "free training", "book a call", "join the program", "free masterclass", "comment X for the link", "apply now", etc.
- The combination above is enough to confidently say this is a coach/consultant/expert selling something

Examples that qualify on bio alone:
- "Hormone coach | Helping women in perimenopause | Free training ↓"
- "Business mentor for 6-fig agency owners | DM 'GROW' for info"
- "Mindset coach | 1:1 + group programs | Apply via link"
- "Sales trainer to 7-figure consultants | Free masterclass below"

### Mark "disqualified" (clear DQ from bio) when ANY of:

1. **Private account** — `is_private: true`. They can't market publicly.

2. **Pure product / e-commerce brand** — supplements, clothing, equipment, food products. No service or coaching component visible.

3. **SaaS / software / mobile app** — primary offering is software/tools/apps, even if founder has personal brand.

4. **Local-service-only business** — dentists, salons, restaurants, gyms (no online programs visible), auto repair, plastic surgery practices, real estate agents selling houses.

5. **Pure media / entertainment / news / influencer** — personal lifestyle, fashion blogs, news outlets, entertainment channels, sponsored-content creators with no paid product of their own. Includes celebrity verified accounts whose monetization is sponsorships.

6. **Affiliate marketer (as primary)** — bio explicitly says "affiliate marketer" or shows known affiliate-only patterns.

7. **Non-commercial** — churches, non-profits, government agencies, community groups with no paid offering.

8. **Enterprise-only B2B** — bio explicitly emphasizes "Fortune 500", "Enterprise", "RFP", names only major corporate clients.

9. **Regulated financial services** — investment management, ETF sponsors, fund managers, brokerage, RIA, insurance sales, lending, AUM-based financial planning. (BUT: financial educators selling courses/coaching about money WITHOUT advisory licenses → "qualified" or "unclear", not DQ.)

10. **Service-delivery agencies (non-marketing, non-consulting)** — recruitment/staffing, talent/casting, travel agencies, real estate (selling houses), AI implementation/build agencies, dev shops, video production, translation. RULE: if they sell labor/execution → DQ. If they sell strategy/expertise → qualified.

11. **Non-English bio** — bio is primarily in a non-English language. (English bio with multilingual audience is fine.)

12. **No commercial intent** — pure personal account, no business/service/offering language at all. "Mom of 3 living my best life" with no offer = DQ. "Just a guy who loves travel" = DQ.

### Mark "unclear" when none of the above strongly applies and bio alone isn't enough:
- Vague identity claim ("Mindset coach" with no offer language)
- Strong claim but no funnel signal ("I help women find their purpose" with no CTA or offer)
- Multi-niche or unclear positioning
- Has external URL but bio doesn't reveal what they sell
- Therapist-adjacent (might be licensed practitioner with online programs OR just a therapist taking only in-person clients — investigate)
- Spiritual/healer with bio that's mystical-leaning but no clear offer description

## DEFAULT STANCE
When in doubt, choose "unclear" rather than "qualified" or "disqualified". The next stage will fetch the external URL and resolve the ambiguity. False-DQs are costly (we lose real leads); false-qualifies are also costly (we waste outreach budget). "Unclear" is a safe routing decision.
